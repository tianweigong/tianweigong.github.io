Books & Videos in Cognitive Science
Popular Science Books
New, cognitive science relevant, empirical approach, funny, suitable for English reading beginners.
  • Scienceblind: Why Our Intuitive Theories About the World Are So Often Wrong (by Andrew Shtulman)
    • To learn about intuitive theories we hold conflicting with the reality (at least with up-to-date scientific theories). Also to gain a basic sense of how scientists do infant or child experiments.
  • Algorithms to Live By: The Computer Science of Human Decisions (by Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths)
    • A book about how computer algorithms can be applied to our everyday life. Totally understandable even without any algorithm knowledge before. A good way to learn some popular concepts in computational cognitive science.
  • The Knowledge Illusion: Why We Never Think Alone (by Steven Sloman & Philip Fernbach)
    • The discovery, reflection, and solution about our failure to "know how little we know about the world". A relative talk from Youtube or Bilibili.
  • The Mind Club: Who Thinks, What Feels, and Why It Matters (by Daniel M. Wegner & Kurt Gray)
    • Do robots have minds? It depends! Welcome to the club and hope you enjoy stories from different members.
  • Your Brain is a Time Machine (by Dean Buonomano)
    • Recommended by my supervisor Neil. Thanks for let me know how interesting the time is!
Tutorials, MOOCs, and Blogs
  • Minds and Machines Summer Course, Summer 2015
    • Summer school courses in MIT, cognitive science, computational approach, containing almost all main areas in cognitive science.
  • Center for Brains, Minds and Machines(CBMM)
    • Go to pick some topics you are interested in, lots of flash presentations within 3 mins but very impressive.
  • Probabilistic Models of Cognition(WebPPL)
    • Powerful computational cognitive language created by Noah Goodman, Tenenbaum and the ProbMods contributors.
  • Writing Workshop (by Barbara Sarnecka)
    • A blog about how to write academic papers in cognitive science/psychology. My favorite chapter: Resistance.
  • CogSci2018 Workshop: Understanding Exploration-Exploitation Trade-offs
    • Talks about one popular topic in cognitive science.
  • Beginner’s CoCoSci list (by Yuan Meng)
    • Best list for cocosci beginners.
  • Bloggingheads.tv
    • Split-screen dialogues about politics, science, philosophy and culture.
  • A Five-Minute Guide to Ph.D. Program Applications (by Philip Guo)
    • A great guide to Ph.D. Applications.
  • Quals Reading Notes (by Jessica Hamrick)
    • Knowed from Yuan. Best notes!
More about Causality or Causal Reasoning
  • The Book of Why: The New Science of Cause and Effect (by Judea Pearl)
    • popular science book. learn about how to judge the causal relationship scientifically (much more than randomizated experiments). If you like it, go on to see other two books from Judea Pearl: Causality, Causal Inference in Statistics.
  • The Oxford Handbook of Causal Reasoning
    • A collection of research about human‘s causal reasoning. Chapters freely available:
    • 1.Causal Reasoning: An Introduction (link for chapter 1-5)
    • 2.Associative Accounts of Causal Cognition
    • 3.Rules of Causal Judgment
    • 4.The Inferential Reasoning Theory of Causal Learning
    • 5.Causal Invariance as an Essential Constraint for Creating a Causal Representation of the World
    • 8.Causal mechanisms
    • 9.Force dynamics
    • 13.Cognitive Neuroscience of Causal Reasoning
    • 27.Intuitive theories
    • 29.Causation in Legal and Moral Reasoning
    • 31.Causality and causal reasoning in natural language
  • A counterfactual simulation model of causal judgments
    • A talk about counterfactual thinking from Tobias Gerstenberg.
