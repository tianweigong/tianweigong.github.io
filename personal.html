Books & Videos in Cognitive Science
Pop Science Books
New, cognitive science relevant, empirical approaches, fun, suitable for English reading beginners.
  • Scienceblind: Why Our Intuitive Theories About the World Are So Often Wrong (by Andrew Shtulman)
    • To learn about intuitive theories we hold conflicting with the reality (at least with up-to-date scientific theories). Also to gain a basic sense of how scientists do infant or child experiments.
  • Algorithms to Live By: The Computer Science of Human Decisions (by Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths)
    • A book about how computer algorithms can be applied to our everyday life. Totally understandable even without any algorithm knowledge. A good way to learn some popular concepts in computational cognitive science.
  • The Knowledge Illusion: Why We Never Think Alone (by Steven Sloman & Philip Fernbach)
    • The discovery, reflection, and solution about our failure to "know how little we know about the world". A relative talk from Youtube or Bilibili.
  • The Mind Club: Who Thinks, What Feels, and Why It Matters (by Daniel M. Wegner & Kurt Gray)
    • Do robots have minds? It depends! Welcome to the club and hope you enjoy stories from different members.
  • Your Brain is a Time Machine: The Neuroscience and Physics of Time (by Dean Buonomano)
    • Recommended by my supervisor Neil. Thanks for let me know how interesting the time is!
  • The Secret of Our Success: How Culture Is Driving Human Evolution, Domesticating Our Species, and Making Us Smarter (by Joseph Henrich)
    • How cultures help humans survive.
